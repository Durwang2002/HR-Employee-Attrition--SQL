Objective: Provide actionable recommendations based on the SQL analysis to help HR reduce employee attrition and improve workforce management.

1. Reduce Employee Attrition in High-Risk Departments

Focus retention initiatives on departments with the highest attrition rates by conducting regular employee feedback sessions and identifying department-specific issues.

2. Review Overtime Policies

Employees working overtime are more likely to leave. Monitor overtime hours, redistribute workloads where possible, and encourage healthier work schedules.

3. Improve Employee Satisfaction

Increase employee engagement through:

Regular feedback meetings
Recognition and rewards
Career development opportunities
Employee wellness programs
4. Enhance Work-Life Balance

Promote flexible working arrangements, leave planning, and wellness initiatives to improve employee well-being and reduce burnout.

5. Strengthen Career Growth Programs

Employees with limited promotion opportunities may become disengaged. Introduce:

Internal promotions
Learning & development programs
Leadership training
Career progression plans
6. Review Compensation Strategy

Benchmark salaries across departments and job roles to ensure competitive and equitable compensation, especially for roles with higher attrition.

7. Develop Targeted Retention Programs

Identify high-risk employee segments (e.g., employees working overtime with low job satisfaction) and create personalized retention plans.

8. Monitor HR KPIs Regularly

Track key metrics such as:

Attrition Rate
Job Satisfaction
Work-Life Balance
Overtime Percentage
Average Employee Tenure

Regular monitoring enables proactive decision-making.

9. Invest in Manager Development

Managers play a critical role in employee retention. Provide leadership and communication training to improve team engagement and workplace culture.
